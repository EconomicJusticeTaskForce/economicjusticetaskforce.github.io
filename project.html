PROJECTS

Current and past projects support survivors of sexual and domestic violence through economic security, education, and recovery initiatives.

Current Projects

On the morning of June 5, 2024, in Worcester, we are convening an event for alternative viewpoints and changing beliefs to address one of the most divisive topics within the field of sexual and domestic violence: sex work. Survivors, including those in recovery settings, need us to come to a deeper understanding of the complex intersection of sex work, survival sex, trafficking, and substance use. This forum will foster open dialogue, challenge preconceptions, and promote a deeper understanding of the nuanced realities of human trafficking, survival sex, sex work, and substance use. More details and registration coming soon.

Past Projects

Formerly known as the Rural Economic Security Initiative or Task Force for Survivors of Domestic and Sexual Violence in Western MA, we have seeded and supported these projects:

  • Elizabeth Freeman Center’s Money School Program for survivors of sexual and domestic violence, helping over 500 rural survivors: https://elizabethfreemancenter.org/learn-more/moneyschool/
  • The Survivor Recovery Fund for survivors of sexual and domestic violence, giving out $20,000 of donation money in matched savings and grants at critical economic turning points in people’s lives: https://economicsecurityforsurvivors.weebly.com/
  • Under Five Thrive for pregnant and parenting young people who need their Hi-Set (aka GED): https://www.bhninc.org/services-and-programs/counseling-wellness/children-youth-family
  • Seeds of Hope provides wrap around care and stipends for EMT training for rural youth ages 17-24 who are graduating high school but not yet going to college and who need to begin economic independence
  • Economic Security Rights and Resource Guide, 2015, the first in Massachusetts, available on the initiative’s website, which was supported by Grant No. 2011-WR-AX-0016 and Grant No. 2014-WR-AX-0011 awarded by the Office on Violence Against Women, U.S. Department of Justice: https://economicsecurityforsurvivors.weebly.com/resource-guides.html
  • Making Change: Storytelling for Transformation gave survivors of sexual and domestic violence tell their truth about money, power, and resilience in the rural Quaboag Hills of western Massachusetts. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k-hOeSk0cvA&t=276s

We have also convened trainings which include:
