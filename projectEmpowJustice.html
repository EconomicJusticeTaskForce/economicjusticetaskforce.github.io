Current Projects - Talk on ‘Empowerment and justice’

On the morning of June 5, 2024, in Worcester, we are convening an event for alternative viewpoints and changing beliefs to address one of the most divisive topics within the field of sexual and domestic violence: sex work. Survivors, including those in recovery settings, need us to come to a deeper understanding of the complex intersection of sex work, survival sex, trafficking, and substance use. This forum will foster open dialogue, challenge preconceptions, and promote a deeper understanding of the nuanced realities of human trafficking, survival sex, sex work, and substance use.

Participants learned about:

  • Meeting people where they are at
  • Harm reduction and harm elimination
  • Sex work is work
  • People who want to exit trafficking are different than people who are consensually engaged in sex work
  • Behaviors of individuals using survival sex are a double-edged sword
  • Our care cannot be predicated on our comfort
  • We can end much of the harm associated with sex work and substance use if we focus on ending poverty and ensure everyone has housing
  • The coercive dynamics of sex trafficking mirror carceral systems including social services
